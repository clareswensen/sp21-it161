HTML Forms

By Clare Swensen

Forms are the way in which sites gather desired information from the users. It could be as simple as a contact form to obtain names and email addresses for leads. A more complex form may be used for checkout on a commerce site and include more detailed input such as product choices, credit card numbers, billing and shipping addresses. No matter the type, it is crucial to create forms that are user friendly. Proper design can assure you are getting the desired information in a format that is usable. In addition, if a form proves difficult for the user, you could lose them to frustration. It is important to keep the form clear and concise without asking unnecessary questions. Give immediate feedback or error messages to direct user correction of improper input. Providing user accessibility should also have a large influence on how you design. Tactics for providing accessibility include; using proper semantics, offering default values, readable font sizes, well contrasting colors, and help prompts. Choosing an appropriate input field will guarantee your data is usable. Some examples of input fields include; checkboxes, radio buttons, text boxes, and drop down menus. It is always a good idea to let the user know that you have received the requested data. This can be done through feedback on the site or a confirmation email. Another careful consideration when placing a form on a site, is privacy. Don’t ask questions you don’t need and tell the user what happens to their data, reassure them it is being used for good and not evil. Always strive to provide an exceptional user experience. Gaining trust and maintaining a good reputation with your users is important to building your brand and online community. Developing best practices when creating forms can translate directly to the relationship you have with your users. Remember, they are giving you valuable information that can be used to benefit your company.
